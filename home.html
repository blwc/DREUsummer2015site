Vertically Centered Text

  • Arrival in Raleigh

    26 May 2015

    It's 3000 miles from sunny (cold and wet) California to North Carolina. I've lived in Texas, the northeast, Oregon, and California, but this is my first time settling for any amount of time in the southeast.

    When I got in, it was in the upper 80's and very, very muggy. I'm living in some on campus apartments with other REUs. The lab I'll be working in is about 2 miles away, but there are on campus busses (called the WolfLine, everything is wolf-something on campus) as well as bike that you can rent for the summer (aptly called WolfWheels).

    The REU I'll be working with is the IIM-REU, Interactive and Intelligent Media. Coming from a small women's liberal arts college, working at a huge research institution is going to be kind of crazy.

  • Week 1

    3 June 2015

    This week I got my assignment and have been coding for the most part. I am working on writing a data visualization prototype for non-hierarchal populations using the D3 JavaScript library. I've worked with JavaScript a couple times before, for my Computing for Good class and for mongoDB in Intro to Databases. The level at which I'll be using it now is way deeper though. I'm excited to use JavaScript, it is the first weakly typed language I'll have worked in extensively. Though, I've done a fair amount of programming in Ruby.

    There are 10 other IIM-REUs and 8 of us are living in the same dorm. We've made acouple outings, thoguh never with the same sub-set. This week has been a blur of getting to know the others, making sure our apartment is furnished enough to function and getting used to having a 9-5 job.

    For a while, the odds seemed kind of insurmountable, I have to learn a library, develop something that has never really been done before (so not very much existing documentation/StackOverflow help) and making a working prototype that can read data. It sounds like it's not very much on paper, but it's actually quite a bit of work. I finally made myself a Pivotal Tracker and that made life way more managable.
